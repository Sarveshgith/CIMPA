About CIMPA

Our Mission

The CIMPA School 2026 aims to provide advanced training in homological methods and their applications in noncommutative algebra and number theory. This two-week program will bring together leading experts and early-career researchers from around the world, creating a stimulating environment for learning, collaboration, and the exchange of ideas. Participants will benefit from a series of Basic and advanced courses, and problem-solving sessions designed to bridge fundamental concepts with cutting-edge developments in the field. Special emphasis will be placed on fostering interaction between established mathematicians and young researchers, encouraging the development of new collaborations and research directions. By the end of the school, participants will have gained not only technical expertise but also a broader perspective on the current challenges and open problems in homological methods, noncommutative algebra, and number theory. The CIMPA School 2026 thus serves as a platform to inspire innovation and strengthen the international mathematical community.

Teaching Experts

Bernhard Keller

Bernhard Keller is a professor at Université Paris Cité. In his research, he applies representation theory and homological algebra to the study of cluster algebras.

Sujatha Ramdorai

Currently holding a Canada Research Chair position at the University of British Columbia, Canada. Prior to this, was a Professor at Tata Institute of Fundamental research. Areas of interest include Algebraic Number Theory, Iwasawa theory of elliptic curves over noncommutative p-adic Lie extensions.

Ashish K Srivastava

Ashish K Srivastava is a Professor of Mathematics at Saint Louis University, USA. His research interests include Noncommutative Algebra, and Combinatorial Algebra. He is an author of two research monographs: one published in the London Math Society Lecture Notes series of Cambridge University Press and the other in Oxford Mathematical Monographs Series of Oxford University Press. Dr Srivastava has published more than 45 research articles, and his research has been partially funded by Simons Foundation. He has served in the American Mathematical Society committee on human rights of mathematicians and is currently serving on the editorial board of the Journal of Algebra and its Applications.

Sanoli Gun

Sanoli Gun is a Professor of Mathematics at the Institute of Mathematical Sciences. She works in Number Theory and in particular, modular forms, special values of L-functions, transcendence and analytic theory of algebraic numbers.

Srikanth B. Iyengar

Srikanth Iyengar grew up in Hyderabad, India, and was granted a Bachelor of Technology in Computer Science, by the Indian Institute of Technology at Madras. He went on to study Mathematics, and earned an M.S. (1994) and a Ph. D. (1998) from Purdue University, West Lafayette. After various post-doctoral positions, and a long stint as a faculty member at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, he joined the Department of Mathematics at the University of Utah in 2014. Srikanth's main research interest is commutative algebra, but he often borrows techniques from algebraic topology and representation theory. He has also returned that favor by applying ideas from commutative algebra to solve problems arising in the modular representation theory of finite groups, and, more recently, in algebraic number theory.

Karin Baur

Originally from Switzerland, Karin Baur is a professor at the Ruhr University Bochum. My research lies in algebraic, geometric and combinatorial methods in representation theory. Current research interests are in cluster algebras and cluster categories, in frieze patterns, surface combinatorics.

Umamaheswaran Arunachalam

Umamaheswaran Arunachalam is an Assistant Professor of Mathematics at SASTRA Deemed University, Thanjavur. His research interests include study of Rings and categories of Modules, Homological Algebra, Cluster Algebras, Lie algebras, Representation theory of finite dimensional algebras.
